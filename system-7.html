12–24 Month Plan Active Development

Future Development
Roadmap — KDExpress Canada

Four planned systems building directly on the infrastructure already deployed. All developments are extensions of existing architecture — continuity in system design ensures effective implementation.

Why Continued Involvement Is Essential

All planned developments are direct extensions of systems already built and currently in active use. The architectural structure, data flows, and operational logic established in existing systems form the foundation for future development.

Continuity in system design and implementation ensures that new components remain fully aligned with operational workflows and financial processes — reducing the risk of fragmentation as the company scales.

Planned Projects
1

KDExpress Hub — Centralised AI Knowledge Base

6 months

KDExpress Canada currently manages pricing, operational procedures, and business data across multiple disconnected sources. Staff frequently require manual assistance to locate up-to-date information, and new employees require significant onboarding time.

Natural language query
Staff retrieve accurate, company-specific information through conversational interface
Automated notifications
Internal updates broadcast automatically when data changes — reduces manual coordination
Self-service onboarding
New employees access structured knowledge base — reduces dependency on senior staff
Tech stack: RAG architecture, Vector database, LLM integration, existing KDExpress data sources
2

Zalo CRM & AI-Powered Customer Service Automation

12–15 months

Customer service at KDExpress Canada currently relies on manual responses to high volumes of routine enquiries via Zalo. A significant portion of these enquiries are repetitive and follow predictable patterns — suitable for AI-assisted handling.

Centralised interface
Multiple Zalo accounts consolidated into a single management platform
AI-assisted responses
Routine enquiries handled automatically — tracking, pricing, delivery status
Smart routing
Complex or sensitive cases routed to human CS staff — maintains service quality
Tech stack: Zalo API, LLM integration, existing WMS-CFS customer data, Node.js backend
3

Platform Rebuild & System Consolidation

6 months

The current logistics platform manages multiple service types within a single structure, resulting in performance limitations and reduced flexibility as operational scale increases.

Service separation
Separate service modules for improved performance, scalability, and independent deployment
UX improvement
Enhanced user experience for warehouse staff and customers — faster workflows
Unified architecture
Integrate all existing and new systems into a single scalable infrastructure
Tech stack: React.js, Node.js, PostgreSQL — modular microservices architecture
4

AI-Assisted Workflow Automation Across Departments

Up to 24 months

Building on the existing system infrastructure, this initiative expands AI-assisted automation across multiple business functions — progressively reducing repetitive manual tasks and allowing the company to scale without proportional increases in administrative workload.

Operations
Automated scheduling, shipment consolidation, and flight assignment optimisation
Finance
Expanded automation beyond payment reconciliation — cost tracking and financial reporting
Incremental deployment
Each stage validated through real-world usage — aligned with actual operational needs
Tech stack: Builds on existing Power Automate, Google Gemini, React.js, PostgreSQL infrastructure
